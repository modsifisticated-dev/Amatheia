Children of the sylvan celestials and masters of the elf-haunted forests known as wodes, wode elves see all forests as their birthright. They know and take pride in their reputation among humans for snatching children who wander too far into the woods. Humans should fear the trees.

The wode elves’ natural ability to mask their presence, called glamour, complements their guerrilla style of fighting. It allows them to strike swiftly from cover, then melt back into the underbrush. These same traits make the relatively few wode elves who dwell in cities naturally adept at urban warfare.

On Wode Elves:
On High Elves
“I’m scared,” Wenna said. “We should go back.” The forest felt as if it were closing in on them.

“We’re not going back,” Jeremy said. Normally, such a statement would have ended the discussion, but they were alone and far from home.

“What if we’re going in circles?”

“Then we keep going in circles!” Dade called from somewhere up ahead. “Until we find the elves.”

“The elves have found you!” a clear, bright voice called out.

The children froze. They scanned the woods, but there was no sign of the speaker.

Then, only a few feet from them, a half-dozen figures seemed to peel out of the forest itself, as if the trees, bushes, and grass had been painted across their bodies to perfectly match the wode. They wore light armor covered in leaves, moss, and vines, and bristled with weapons.

Meliora gasped. Credan frowned, and Wenna hushed her. Dade was ushered back toward them by two more wode elves, his bow still in hand.

The children huddled together, Credan’s hand clutching the symbol of Saint Gryffyn around his neck, and Jeremy’s resting on the hilt of his sword.

The elves were tall, taller than an adult human, yet they seemed to crouch the moment they stopped moving. Their eyes were unsettling, wide and set far apart. But it was their ears, long, tall, and twisting, shaped to catch every sound, that marked them as elves of the wode.

“Admittedly, though, most Terrans regret the experience.”

The voice came again, this time from above.

The children looked up and saw a wode elf with long, furred, twisting ears and nut-brown skin smiling down at them. They wore a brightly colored outfit that stood out against the forest. The children watched as the elf leapt lightly from branch to branch, descending with effortless grace before landing in a small flourish on the forest floor.

“Consort!” one of the elves said in Yllyric, rising from his crouch and bowing. “We have been tracking these since they entered the wode.”

Meliora, who understood the words, whispered to the others, “They called that one ‘consort!’”

Llyander smiled, glancing from Meliora to the elf who had spoken, wearing a clear _See? I told you_ expression.

The tall, swashbuckling elf bowed to the children. “I am Llyander, the Lightning Strike, consort to Queen Imyrr.” He gestured to the elf who had spoken. “This is my cousin, Rhythylthin.”

“How did you know we were here?” Jeremy asked.

Dade stood just behind him, an arrow now nocked.

Rhythylthin stepped forward and, while Dade’s attention was on the queen’s consort, deftly plucked the arrow from the young man’s bow.

“Nothing happens within the wode without our knowledge,” the elf said.

Dade spun toward him and nocked another arrow.

“And approval,” Rhythylthin added, clenching his fist and snapping the arrow in two.

“You come bearing a gift for our queen. The Codex Dryadalis.” Llyander nodded toward the heavy scroll Meliora carried. “My cousin Rhythylthin was sent to capture you and escort you to the Orchid Court. But I am the queen’s consort, and I have my own thoughts on the matter.”

The elf smiled at the children. “But have no fear,” they said. Their Eforian was perfect, their voice like a melody. “You are safe… now.”

The pause before _now_ spoke volumes.

“Are you a… a… boy or a girl?” Wenna asked.

Llyander smiled gaily. “I am a song! I contain melodies and harmonies alike,” they said.

Wenna smiled.

Rhythylthin rolled his eyes. Llyander caught the gesture and winked at him.

“Fashions change. My cousin here wears the new trends.” They gestured toward the other wode elf’s garb and masculine appearance. “Me? I’m old-fashioned.” They swept a hand over their own bright attire. “Grace never goes out of style.”

Llyander turned to Rhythylthin and the rest of the wode elf band. “Their gift goes to Lord Tear, methinks. I will escort them.” Then, suddenly imperious, they added, “You may go.”

Despite his earlier skepticism toward the queen’s consort, Rhythylthin straightened and bowed.

As one, the elves turned and flowed into the wode. Within only a few steps, they melted into the trees and undergrowth.

“How did they do that?” Meliora demanded, spinning to confront their benefactor.

“Hmm? Do what?” Llyander asked, glancing after the departing elves.

“Just… disappear like that!”

Llyander looked at the other children with a mix of wonder and mild annoyance. “Do Terran children not play hiding games?”

“Well…” Jeremy looked at Dade, who offered no help. “We do, but…”

Llyander made a theatrical, dismissive gesture. “Then it is the same thing. But for our people, it is a game we practice all our lives. We would be poor protectors of the wode if we could not conceal ourselves within it.”

“But that was…” Meliora struggled for the words. “That was magic!”

“You say? Well,” Llyander mused, “Terrans are a part and apart, it is said. It is your blessing and your curse, methinks. Perhaps someday you can explain it to me.”

Their eyes twinkled at Meliora’s frustration.

Llyander turned and marched off. “Come!” they called.

The children hurried to catch up.

“Where are we going?” Wenna asked.

“I enjoy the favor of Lord Tear,” Llyander said. “We are old friends. With me as your guide, he will treat you well, likely bestow favor upon you. You should be in anticipation of great treasure.” They smiled.

The elf stopped suddenly and spun toward them, serious now, but not unkind. They pointed to each of the children in turn.

“I will instruct you on the proper etiquette, but remember this. Lord Tear will test us. Some tests for you, and some for me. The high elves and the wode elves are distant cousins. You will hear much that is polite, much that is flattering, but it is all another kind of glamour. It hides deep tensions, recently worsened by the treaty with Amergin.”

The children nodded.

Satisfied, Llyander turned and continued on, and they followed.

Jeremy leaned toward Dade. “I feel like we’re in a dream,” he whispered.

“You are!” their escort called back. “The wode is a dream. With a little luck, one you may soon wake safely from.”
