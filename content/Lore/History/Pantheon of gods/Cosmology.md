There are two types of gods:
[[Gods of Man]]
[[Primordial Gods]]

The entire world exists within the dream of [[The All-Father]].  
Long ago, he was tricked into an endless sleep by the trickster god [[Eostre]]. At that time, the world was nothing but air and possibility, without form or substance.

[[Eostre]] deceived the other gods, convincing them that their domains should rule over existence. Through this trick, he bound them to the responsibility of shaping the world and caring for the lives of humans.

Each of the elemental gods spread their domain across the empty dream (Earth, Air, Fire, Water, Light, and Darkness) until the world took form.

In public, people worship only [[Eostre]], for fear that he will grow jealous and wake the All-Father.  

However, inside their churches, people secretly pray to the other gods. All churches are built to look identical from the outside, so that no god may know which deity is truly being worshipped within. Inside, each church is dedicated to a different god.

The elemental gods resent their role as rulers of the new world, yet they continue to be worshipped for the aspects of their domains. They fear that if they displease [[Eostre]], he will place them into the same endless sleep as he did the All-Father.

In time, the emotions and desires of humans gave birth to new gods, the Gods of Man.  
These gods were not created by the elements, but by mortal thought, feeling, and belief.
